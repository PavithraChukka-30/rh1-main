# Air Canvas for Hand Rehabilitation

## (SDG 3: Good Health & Well‑Being)

---

## 1. Project Overview

**Air Canvas for Hand Rehabilitation** is a low‑cost, touchless rehabilitation support system that tracks and analyzes hand movement using a simple webcam and computer vision techniques. The system allows users to draw shapes in the air using finger movements while objectively measuring improvement over time.

This project is designed as a **supportive rehabilitation aid**, not a medical diagnostic or treatment tool.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

In many rehabilitation centers, especially in rural and low‑resource areas:

* Patient progress is observed manually by therapists
* Feedback is often subjective ("slight improvement", "better control")
* There is no numerical or visual tracking of recovery
* Therapists are overworked and cannot monitor detailed movement every session
* Daily travel to rehab centers is difficult for many patients

This creates a gap between **actual recovery progress** and **what can be measured or documented**.

---

## 3. Proposed Solution

The proposed system solves this gap by:

* Tracking hand and finger movement using a webcam
* Allowing patients to perform simple air‑drawing exercises
* Automatically recording motion data
* Calculating objective metrics such as stability, smoothness, and completion time
* Showing progress visually through graphs and reports

This makes rehabilitation **measurable, engaging, and accessible**.

---

## 4. Core Features (Must‑Have)

These features are sufficient for a complete and strong mini‑project.

### 4.1 Hand Tracking

* Uses **MediaPipe Hands** for real‑time hand landmark detection
* Tracks fingertip position accurately
* Works with a single standard webcam

### 4.2 Air Drawing

* User draws shapes (circle, line, square) in the air
* Fingertip path is plotted on screen
* Feels interactive and game‑like

### 4.3 Path Tracking

* Stores x, y coordinates of fingertip movement
* Maintains motion history for each session

### 4.4 Performance Metrics

* **Stability** – measures hand shakiness
* **Smoothness** – measures consistency of motion
* **Completion Time** – time taken to complete a task

### 4.5 Progress Reporting

* Session‑wise comparison (Day 1 vs Day 7)
* Visual graphs showing improvement
* Numerical scores instead of subjective comments

| Hand Tracking     | Easy       | MediaPipe provides landmarks   |
| Air Drawing       | Easy       | Plot fingertip coordinates     |
| Stability Metric  | Medium     | Standard deviation calculation |
| Smoothness Metric | Medium     | Velocity variance              |
| Accuracy Scoring  | Medium     | Path comparison                |
| Dashboard         | Medium     | Basic charts                   |

No part of the project requires heavy deep learning or medical AI.

---

## 7. Example Metric Logic

### 7.1 Hand Stability

* Less shaking = better stability
* Calculated using variation in fingertip position

### 7.2 Motion Smoothness

* Smooth motion = consistent speed
* Sudden jerks reduce smoothness score

### 7.3 Accuracy

* Compares user‑drawn path with ideal shape
* Higher overlap = higher accuracy

All metrics use **basic mathematics**, not complex AI models.

## 8. Real‑World Use and Impact

### 8.1 Objective Progress Tracking

Instead of vague feedback:

* Stability score: 62 → 78
* Smoothness score: 55 → 70
* Completion time reduced

Numbers and graphs provide clarity and confidence.

### 8.2 Improved Patient Engagement

* Rehab exercises often feel boring
* Air drawing feels like a game
* Increased motivation leads to better practice consistency

### 8.3 Support for Therapists

* Automatic session recording
* Reports can be reviewed later
* Saves therapist time and effort

### 8.4 Remote & Home‑Based Rehabilitation

* Patients can practice from home
* Data can be reviewed remotely
* Highly useful for rural and mobility‑restricted patients
